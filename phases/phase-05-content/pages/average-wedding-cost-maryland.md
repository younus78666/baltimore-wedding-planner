Couples I've worked with in Baltimore spend anywhere from $8,500 for an intimate 30-person ceremony to $45,000 for a full 150-person reception with all the extras.

What matters is where in Maryland you're marrying, how many guests you're inviting, and which vendors you're prioritizing. This guide breaks it all down with real numbers.

## What Is the Average Wedding Cost in Maryland?

The average wedding cost in Maryland is $24,000 for couples who spend at the middle of the market. Budget-focused couples typically spend $8,000 to $15,000, while mid-range weddings run $15,000 to $30,000, and luxury celebrations start at $35,000.

Here's how Maryland compares to the national picture:

| Tier | Maryland | National Average |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Budget | $8,000 to $15,000 | $10,000 to $18,000 |
| Mid-Range | $15,000 to $30,000 | $18,000 to $35,000 |
| Luxury | $35,000 to $75,000+ | $40,000 to $100,000+ |

Maryland skews slightly below national averages because Baltimore and Annapolis have more mid-range venue options than cities like New York or San Francisco. That's good news if you want a real celebration without a six-figure price tag and you don't need to move mountains to pull it off.

## Why Do Maryland Wedding Costs Vary So Much?

Maryland wedding costs shift significantly based on location, guest count, and season. Understanding what drives costs up (or down) helps you make smarter tradeoffs.

**Location matters a lot:**
Baltimore City venues tend to run 10 to 25% higher than venues in Frederick, Carroll, or Harford counties for comparable spaces. Annapolis waterfront venues command a premium for the views and demand. Eastern Shore venues often offer the best value, especially weekday and off-season bookings.

**Guest count is the biggest lever:**
Catering typically runs $65 to $110 per person in Maryland's mid-range market. A 30-person wedding costs $1,950 to $3,300 in catering alone. A 100-person wedding costs $6,500 to $11,000 for the same food quality. Your guest list is the single biggest factor in total cost, which is why micro-weddings and elopements save couples $10,000 to $20,000 on average.

**Season affects pricing by 20 to 40%:**
Peak season in Maryland runs May through October. Venues and photographers charge their highest rates during this stretch. A venue that quotes $5,000 for a Saturday in September might rent for $2,500 on a Friday in January. If you're working with a tight budget, a winter or early spring wedding in Maryland can cut your venue and photographer costs dramatically.

**Sunday and Friday weddings save money:**
Most Maryland vendors charge 15 to 25% less for non-Saturday bookings. A Sunday afternoon wedding looks identical to a Saturday celebration in the photos, and it'll cost you significantly less to pull off.

## How Do You Plan a Maryland Wedding Under $20,000?

A $20,000 wedding in Maryland is very doable. I've done it many times. It requires prioritizing ruthlessly and knowing which vendors offer real value at lower price points.

**The levers that matter most at $20,000:**
- Keep the guest list under 75 people. Every additional guest adds $70 to $120 in catering costs.
- Book a non-Saturday date. Fridays and Sundays typically save $1,000 to $3,000 on venue costs.
- Choose a venue that allows outside catering. All-inclusive venues limit your options; BYO-catering venues let you shop for value.
- Skip a live band. A great Maryland DJ runs $1,000 to $1,800 versus $5,000 to $10,000 for a live band.
- Do florals selectively. Focus your floral budget on the ceremony arch and bridal bouquet. Table arrangements can use greenery, candles, and simple blooms instead of elaborate centerpieces.

**What $15,000 looks like with smart planning:**
- Venue: $2,500 (weekday or off-season booking at a mid-range Baltimore venue)
- Catering: $4,500 (50 guests at $90 per person)
- Photography: $2,500 (solid mid-range Baltimore photographer)
- DJ: $1,200
- Florals: $1,000
- Officiant: $350
- Dress and attire: $1,200
- Cake: $450
- Stationery: $300
- Tips and gifts: $500
- Day-of coordinator: $800
- **Total: $15,300**

That's a real wedding with professional photography, catering, and someone managing the day. Not a scaled-down version. Just smart priorities.

## What Does an Elopement or Micro-Wedding Cost in Maryland?

If you want to spend dramatically less, elopements and micro-weddings (under 30 guests) are where the real savings are.

| Type | Typical Cost | What's Included |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Courthouse ceremony | $100 to $250 | License + courthouse fee. That's it. |
| Park elopement | $500 to $2,500 | License, officiant, photographer, location permit |
| Micro-wedding (under 30 guests) | $3,000 to $8,000 | Venue, catering, photographer, florals, officiant |
| Intimate wedding (30 to 50 guests) | $8,000 to $15,000 | Full vendor team, mid-range pricing |

Maryland's elopement-friendly parks (Quiet Waters Park, Patterson Park, Assateague Island) cost $50 to $150 in permits. Add a photographer ($1,500 to $2,500) and a registered officiant ($200 to $400), and you've got a legally binding, genuinely beautiful ceremony for under $3,500.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Maryland Wedding Costs

**What's included in the average $24,000 Maryland wedding?**
A $24,000 Maryland wedding typically includes a venue rental ($4,000 to $6,000), catering for 75 to 100 guests ($6,000 to $9,000), a photographer ($2,500 to $3,500), a DJ ($1,500), florals ($1,500 to $2,500), and the remaining budget split across the dress, officiant, cake, stationery, and tips. It's a mid-range celebration with professional vendors across all categories.

**Is it cheaper to get married in Baltimore or Annapolis?**
Baltimore generally offers more budget-friendly venue options than Annapolis. Baltimore's industrial and arts district venues (Clipper Mill, Motor House, The Foundry) tend to run $2,000 to $5,000 for venue rental. Annapolis waterfront venues often start at $4,000 to $7,000 for the same guest capacity. If your heart is set on Annapolis, consider a weekday or off-season date to close the gap.

**How much do couples typically spend on a photographer in Maryland?**
Maryland wedding photographers charge $1,500 to $5,500 depending on experience and package. Budget photographers in the $1,500 to $2,500 range deliver solid work. Established Baltimore and Annapolis photographers with editorial portfolios charge $3,500 to $5,500. Photography is one category where going up one price tier often makes a significant visual difference.

**Does hiring a wedding planner increase or decrease the total cost?**
It depends on the service level. Day-of coordination ($500 to $1,500) adds cost but prevents expensive day-of mistakes. Full-service planning ($1,500 to $2,800) often pays for itself through vendor discounts and avoided mistakes. Most of my full-service clients end up spending less total than they would have without planning help, because I catch budget-killing decisions early.

**What's the cheapest month to get married in Maryland?**
January and February are the least expensive months for Maryland weddings. Venues discount by 20 to 40%, and photographers and caterers are more negotiable on pricing. March and November are also good value options. Peak demand runs June through October, when venues and most vendors charge their highest rates.
