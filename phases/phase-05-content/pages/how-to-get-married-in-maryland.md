## What Are the Legal Requirements to Get Married in Maryland?

To legally get married in Maryland, both people must be at least 18 years old, appear together in person at a Maryland Circuit Court, obtain a valid marriage license, and have the ceremony performed by a registered or ordained officiant in the presence of at least one witness.

**Eligibility checklist:**
- Both applicants must be 18 or older (17 with parental consent in limited cases)
- Neither applicant can be currently married
- Maryland does not allow first-cousin marriages
- No Maryland residency required — out-of-state and international couples are fully eligible

**Documents you need at the Circuit Court:**
- Valid government-issued photo ID for both applicants (driver's license, passport, or state ID)
- Social Security numbers for both applicants
- Certified divorce decree or death certificate if either applicant was previously married
- Both applicants must appear at the same time — no exceptions

**Officiant requirements:**
Maryland recognizes the following officiant categories:
- Judges and magistrates
- Justices of the Peace
- Licensed religious ministers or clergy
- Online-ordained ministers (Universal Life Church and similar ordinations are valid in Maryland)

One witness must be present at the ceremony and sign the license. Most couples bring a second witness for simplicity.

## How Do You Apply for a Maryland Marriage License? Step by Step

Applying for a Maryland marriage license takes under one hour at most Circuit Courts, and most counties issue the license the same day you apply.

**Step 1: Gather your documents.** Both applicants need a valid photo ID, their Social Security number, and — if previously married — a certified copy of the divorce decree or death certificate. The Circuit Court does not accept photocopies for prior marriage documentation.

**Step 2: Choose your Circuit Court.** You can apply at any Maryland Circuit Court regardless of where you live or where the ceremony will take place. Baltimore City Circuit Court is the most central for Baltimore-area couples. Anne Arundel County Circuit Court in Annapolis handles elopement-oriented couples efficiently. Call ahead to confirm hours.

**Step 3: Appear together in person.** Both applicants must visit the Circuit Court at the same time. You cannot apply separately or send a representative.

**Step 4: Complete the application and pay the fee.** The clerk processes your application in about 20-30 minutes. Most courts accept cash and credit cards. Fees range from $65 to $85 depending on the county.

**Step 5: Receive your license.** Most Maryland counties issue the license immediately on the same day. Montgomery and Prince George's counties have historically required a 48-hour wait between application and issuance — call before planning a same-day ceremony.

**Step 6: Hold your ceremony.** Your officiant signs the marriage license during or immediately after the ceremony. You then have 60 days from the ceremony date to return the signed license to the Circuit Court for filing. Do not lose the license — it is the legal record of your marriage.

## Is There a Waiting Period for a Maryland Marriage License?

Most Maryland counties have no waiting period. You can apply for your license and hold your ceremony the same day.

Baltimore City, Anne Arundel, Howard, Frederick, Harford, Carroll, and most rural Maryland counties issue licenses immediately upon application approval. If your ceremony is planned for the same day as your application, any of these counties work without issue.

Montgomery County and Prince George's County have historically required 48 hours between application and license issuance. This can affect couples planning ceremonies in the DC-adjacent suburbs. Apply two days before your ceremony if you use these counties.

**License validity:** A Maryland marriage license is valid for six months from the date of issue. If you don't hold a ceremony within that window, the license expires and you need to apply again. After your ceremony, you have 60 days to file the signed license.

## What Are Your Ceremony Location Options in Maryland?

Once you have a Maryland marriage license, the ceremony can take place anywhere in the state — a courthouse, a public park, a private venue, or any outdoor location you choose.

**Courthouse ceremony:** Many Maryland Circuit Courts offer on-site ceremonies performed by a judge or staff official. This is the most minimal option. Cost is typically $25-$50 for the ceremony fee, separate from the license. Baltimore City and Annapolis both offer courthouse ceremonies. Slots book up on Fridays — schedule at least one week in advance.

**Public park elopement:** Maryland's state and county parks are among the best elopement locations in the mid-Atlantic region. Quiet Waters Park in Annapolis requires a permit for ceremonies ($50-$150 depending on group size and day). Cunningham Falls State Park in Frederick County offers a mountain waterfall setting and permits through the Maryland DNR. Patterson Park in Baltimore City requires no permit for groups under 25 people.

**Private venue:** Any private property works — a backyard, a rented estate, a restaurant private room. The officiant brings the license, signs it during the ceremony, and you file it afterward.

**Destination from out of state:** Maryland is a common destination for out-of-state couples who want a simpler process than their home state offers. The license is issued in Maryland and is nationally recognized once filed.

## What Do You Need to Legally Elope in Maryland?

A legal Maryland elopement requires the same marriage license as any other ceremony, a registered or ordained officiant, and one witness.

Maryland parks and outdoor locations make this state one of the best elopement destinations on the East Coast. Here are six locations I regularly recommend to couples:

**Quiet Waters Park (Annapolis, Anne Arundel County):** Waterfront views of the South River, $50-$100 permit, available year-round. One of Maryland's most popular elopement spots.

**Patterson Park (Baltimore City):** The historic pagoda provides a distinctive backdrop. No permit required for groups under 25 people. Accessible and central.

**Assateague Island National Seashore:** Atlantic Ocean beach ceremony. Requires a National Park Service permit ($150 for ceremonies). Stunning in any season.

**Cunningham Falls State Park (Thurmont, Frederick County):** Maryland's largest waterfall is less than two hours from Baltimore. State park permit required through Maryland DNR.

**Annapolis City Dock:** Public waterfront with sailboat views. No permit required for groups under 10 people. Ceremony in the morning before crowds arrive.

**Oregon Ridge Park (Baltimore County):** Wooded park with open meadows and a small lake. Baltimore County permit required, typically $75.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Getting Married in Maryland

**Can a friend officiate a wedding in Maryland?**
Yes. A friend or family member can legally perform a wedding ceremony in Maryland if they are ordained through a recognized organization. Online ordinations through Universal Life Church are valid in Maryland. The ordained person must be listed on the marriage license as the officiant and must sign the license immediately after the ceremony. Verify with your specific county's Circuit Court at least two weeks before the ceremony, as interpretation can vary.

**How long is a Maryland marriage license valid?**
A Maryland marriage license is valid for six months from the date of issue. The ceremony must take place within this six-month window. After the ceremony, the signed license must be filed with the Circuit Court within 60 days of the ceremony date.

**Can you apply for a Maryland marriage license online?**
No. As of 2026, Maryland requires both applicants to appear in person at a Circuit Court to apply. There is no online application or mail-in option. Both applicants must be present at the same time.

**Do you need to be a Maryland resident to get married in Maryland?**
No. Maryland has no residency requirement for marriage licenses. Out-of-state couples, international couples, and same-sex couples from states with more restrictive laws can all apply at any Maryland Circuit Court and hold their ceremony anywhere in the state.

**How soon after getting the license can you get married in Maryland?**
In most Maryland counties — including Baltimore City, Anne Arundel, Howard, Frederick, Carroll, and Harford — you can get married the same day you receive your license. Montgomery and Prince George's counties require a 48-hour wait between application and issuance. Confirm with the specific Circuit Court before booking your officiant or venue.
